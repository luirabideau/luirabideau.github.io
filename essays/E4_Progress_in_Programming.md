<p><b> My programming skills have greatly improved in the following ways: </b></p>
<p>I am able to program in javascript and be able to finish parts of assignment 1 without help or instruction. </p>

<p><b> I need to work on the following to improve my programming skills:</b></p>
<p>I need to keep going and continue to research things I don’t know. Additionally, I need to really read into the technicalities of the programs.</p>

<p><b> I have learned a lot from doing WODs and I can do them without copying the screencast:</b></p>
<p>Many of the WODs are so simple that you could do them without the screencast and still come to the same answers as the screencast. The last 2 or so WODs were especially difficult but now I think I could do them easily with no help from the screencast.</p>

<p><b> The labs have help me learn and I am able to complete them with confidence:</b></p>
<p>I typically struggle initially with the labs, but by the time I start to work on them/use them for a WOD or assignment, I find that I completely understand them. </p>

<p><b> I learned a lot from my experience doing Assignment 1 and feel ready to work on Assignment 2:</b></p>
<p>I learned a great deal from assignment 1, and I do feel ready to work on assignment 2. However, I think my experiences in other coding/technical electronics classes has helped me feel prepared. </p>

<p><b> I think the class can be improved to help my learning in the following ways:</b></p>
<p>Every WOD should have a screencast. Some of the labs can be shortened. All of the labs need more clear instructions. There should be more of an emphasis on using exact terms, asking exact questions, thinking like a programmer, and in-code comments. Additionally, the class website needs to be fixed (spelling errors, grammatical errors, multiple pages for the same lab etc). </p>

<p><b> What helps me the most in class are (WODs, Labs, class website, screencasts, readings, quizzes, one on one help, working with classmates, etc.):</b></p>
<p><b> Working with classmates</b></p>
<p>Neither my classmates nor I would know the answer, but having people to go to office hours/ask dumb questions with, really made a huge difference.</p>
<p><b> Labs </b></p>
<p>Everything in the labs corresponds to the assignments. Every lab is important, although the order doesn’t always make sense. </p>
<p><b> WODs </b></p>
<p>This is where the learning really takes place. I can imagine that many may not like these, but I really enjoyed just sitting down and coding for a few hours. 
<p><b> One on one help </b></p>
<p>Professor Port might be the most knowledgeable programmer I’ve ever met. You can tell how experienced he is just by listening to him. He knows javascript startlingly well and is very inspirational.  </p>
<p><b> Readings </b></p>
<p>These were especially helpful when the course first started. There is really no other way to learn the syntax of JavaScript/HTML/CSS without reading about it. </p>
<p><b> Quizzes </b></p>
<p>These were fantastic, I think they are a good test to see how well I processed the readings. </p>
<p><b> Class website </b></p>
<p>Arguably, the class website worked more against me than for me. The due dates were confusing and many of the instructions were unclear. </p>
